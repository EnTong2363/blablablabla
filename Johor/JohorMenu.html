Johor

Johor Bahru (JB), Malaysia's southernmost and second-largest city, is home to over 2.7 million people, including its suburbs. The ongoing Iskandar megaproject aims to make JB the country's largest financial hub. Connected to Singapore by two bridges, JB attracts around 16 million tourists annually. Many residents work in Singapore for better opportunities, while Singaporeans visit JB for affordable shopping. As a key industrial, tourism, and commercial center, JB boasts one of Southeast Asia's highest population growth rates and is part of the economically thriving Sijori Growth Triangle.
