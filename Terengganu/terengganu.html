Explore Terengganu

Discover the natural wonders and rich cultural heritage of Terengganu, Malaysia. Enjoy the breathtaking coastal landscapes and vibrant traditions of this unique state.

What About Terengganu

Terengganu, located on the east coast of Peninsular Malaysia, is famous for its stunning islands, traditional crafts, and vibrant cultural heritage. The state boasts some of the most beautiful beaches in Malaysia, making it a favorite destination for tourists seeking sun, sea, and serenity.

Terengganu is a haven for nature lovers and cultural enthusiasts alike. Whether you're exploring the pristine waters of Redang Island or discovering the historic sites in Kuala Terengganu, there's something for everyone in this picturesque state.

The people of Terengganu are known for their warm hospitality and deep-rooted traditions. Visitors can experience local culture through the state's unique festivals, traditional dances, and exquisite handicrafts, such as batik and songket weaving.

One of Terengganu's highlights is the Crystal Mosque, a modern architectural marvel that glows beautifully at night. Additionally, the Terengganu State Museum, one of the largest museums in Southeast Asia, offers a glimpse into the state's rich history and cultural heritage.

Attractions

Pulau Redang

Pulau Redang is one of the largest islands off the east coast of Malaysia, renowned for its crystal-clear waters, vibrant coral reefs, and luxurious resorts. It's a paradise for snorkeling and diving enthusiasts.

Crystal Mosque

The Crystal Mosque, located in Kuala Terengganu, is a stunning structure made of steel, glass, and crystal. It is not only a place of worship but also a major tourist attraction, especially when it is illuminated at night.

Chinatown

Chinatown in Kuala Terengganu offers a glimpse into the state’s Chinese heritage. The area is filled with historic shophouses, vibrant markets, and delicious street food, making it a must-visit for culture enthusiasts.

Muzium Negeri Terengganu

The Terengganu State Museum is the largest museum in Southeast Asia. It features extensive exhibits on the state's history, culture, and natural heritage, housed in traditional Terengganu-style buildings.
