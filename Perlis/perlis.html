Explore Perlis

Discover the natural wonders and diverse cultures of Perlis, Malaysia. Enjoy the breathtaking landscapes and vibrant traditions of this unique state.

What About Perlis

Perlis, the smallest state in Malaysia, is located in the northern part of the Peninsular Malaysia, bordering Thailand. Despite its size, Perlis is rich in natural beauty and cultural heritage, offering a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Perlis is known for its scenic paddy fields, serene rural landscapes, and historic sites. Visitors can explore the limestone hills, visit the famous Snake and Reptile Farm, or take a relaxing drive through the countryside to experience the charm of this quaint state.

The people of Perlis are known for their friendliness and deep-rooted traditions. Visitors can enjoy local cultural experiences, including traditional dances, local crafts, and the unique flavors of Perlis cuisine.

One of Perlis's highlights is Gua Kelam, a limestone cave known for its mysterious atmosphere and underground river. The state is also home to the Royal Palace of Perlis, a symbol of the state's royal heritage.

Attractions

Gua Kelam

Gua Kelam is one of Perlis's most famous attractions, known for its dark limestone cave and an underground river. It's a popular spot for adventure seekers and nature enthusiasts.

Wang Kelian

Wang Kelian, located near the Thailand border, offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. It's a great spot for hiking, camping, and experiencing the beauty of nature.

Snake and Reptile Farm

The Snake and Reptile Farm in Perlis is the largest of its kind in Malaysia, housing a variety of snakes, crocodiles, and other reptiles. It's a must-visit for those interested in wildlife.

Royal Palace of Perlis

The Royal Palace of Perlis, also known as Istana Arau, is a symbol of the state's royal heritage. The palace is an architectural marvel and offers insights into the history and culture of Perlis.
