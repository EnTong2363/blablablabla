History of Concubine Lane

Concubine Lane, also known as Panglima Lane, is a short alleyway connecting Jalan Panglima and Jalan Market in Ipoh Old Town. It's a small area that can be explored in 30 minutes. Historically, this lane was infamous as the place where wealthy tin mining tycoons, like Yau Tet Shin, kept their mistresses. In Cantonese, Concubine Lane is called Yee Lai Hong, which literally means Mistress Lane, a name that harks back to over a century ago. Nowadays, the lane is bustling with small, tightly packed stalls offering quirky snacks, souvenirs, and trendy clothing.

Ipoh Heritage Trail

Furthermore, you'll likely pass through Concubine Lane if you want to complete the Ipoh Heritage Trail. Concubine Lane is close to the Birch Memorial Clock Tower and other historical landmarks.

More Information

Concubine Lane is a vibrant destination known for its street art, food, and festive decorations, particularly during Chinese New Year. Just 600 meters from Ipoh Railway Station, this lane attracts foodies, bloggers, and photographers, blending history with modern charm. Visitors are encouraged to explore on foot to fully experience its unique appeal.
